NDT-Compressive Strength Estimator

This application generates predictions of the concrete compressive strength based on non-destructive test (NDT) results obtained either in the lab or in the field. The compression strength is normalized to a 300x150 mm reference cylinder. The predictions are generated by CatBoost regression machine learning models, optimized with a tree-structured parzen estimator (TPE) and trained through a 10-fold cross-validation approach. The final predictions are taken as the average value from the 10 models.

The models are trained and tested on a database of 5,680 test results from 83 published studies for UPV, 9,824 test results from 81 studies for RH, and 2,980 test results from 47 studies for SonReb. The final optimized models produce an average mean absolute error (MAE) of 2.92 MPa, 5.27 MPa and 2.18 MPa for the UPV, RH, and SonReb, respectively.

The models are trained on a wide range of concrete types, including normal, high-performance, and self-compacting concrete. The 5th - 95th fractiles for the training data domains are 8.5 - 59.6 MPa for UPV, 11.7 - 85.0 MPa for RH, and 11.3 - 58.7 MPa for SonReb. Outside of these ranges, the models must extrapolate, which may lead to less accurate predictions.

Several fields are optional to fit situations where this information is unknown. However, the more information that is included before submission, the stronger the models will perform and the more accurate the prediction.
